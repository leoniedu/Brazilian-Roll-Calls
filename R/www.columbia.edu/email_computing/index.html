Columbia University Information Technology (CUIT)
Columbia University Information Technology (CUIT) provides computing and communications systems, services, and support to faculty, students, and administrators. CUIT services include a support center, network access, telephony, email, desktop and LAN services for staff and faculty, business systems, academic computer labs, public access kiosks, and web services.

Medical Center Campus Technology Services
Columbia University Medical Center Information Technology (CUMC IT)
CUMC IT provides network services in Medical Center buildings and Residence Halls, the Medical Center Help Desk, support for Medical Center academic computer labs and public access kiosks, support for Medical Center faculty, staff and student desktops, server support, and Web services.

Other University-Wide Technology Services
Center for New Media Teaching and Learning (CCNMTL)
CCNMTL enhances teaching and learning through the purposeful use of new media and technology. Services are free for faculty and instructors and include the creation of CourseWorks websites, wikis, and web-based projects. CCNMTL also hosts Columbia's iTunes U and YouTube channels.

Center for Digital Research and Scholarship (CDRS)
CDRS serves the digital research and scholarly communication needs of the faculty, students, and scholars of Columbia University through creation and implementation of online tools and services.
